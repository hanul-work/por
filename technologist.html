SOLARIS

Social Sonic Interface where Music Interweaves Vision

Interactive Audiovisual Max for Live Plug-In for Automatic VJing on Ableton Live



SOLARIS is an audiovisual production interface that offers a direct and simultaneous interaction between the audio and the visual elements in the computer studio. The audio manipulates the data of the videos and modifies the visuals according to its output levels. SOLARIS eliminates the producer's burden to control every aspect of data-modification in video editing and allows the user's music to edit the video clips in postdigital, deconstructive aesthetics.

The user produces music and imports two videos into the interface. Based on the compressor decibel of the audio piece, the sound modifies the data of the visuals in three different ways while editing them into a singular visual piece. Every time the music reaches the certain audio threshold, the imported video clips add, subtract, or divide the visual data in turn. The user can adjust the audio threshold as well as control which of the three options to be operated, by using the buttons on the interface.

Created for one of the most extensive and well-designed music production software Ableton Live in the sonic-focused multimedia coding language MAX/MSP, SOLARIS aims to expand the experience of any multimedia technologists and artists who create their work with the mentioned programs. SOLARIS can be used as a VJ tool or an editing tool for experimental video or audio artists, who seek to add postdigital, deconstructed aesthetics to their works. It is also a welcoming interface for any users of Ableton Live and MAX/MSP who want to study the close interaction between the audio and visual elements in a multimedia software.

SOLARIS is a tool worth utilizing for any creative minds who are eager to explore some of the countless possibilities of the audiovisual interactions offered by digital technologies.
